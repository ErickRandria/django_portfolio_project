The DARA Big Data project

The project is led by Prof. Anna Scaife of the Jodrell Bank Centre for Astrophysics (JBCA) at the University of Manchester and collaborates with partner organizations in South Africa and universities across the UK (Leeds, Hertfordshire, Sussex, York). DARA Big Data aims to ensure that African students can fully engage in the technological advancements associated with the Fourth Industrial Revolution (4IR)—a global shift towards extensive use of AI, robotics, and smart devices connected to the Internet of Things. By providing intensive skills training, young people across the continent will significantly benefit from the vast opportunities presented by both 4IR and the SKA.


DARA Big Data was established in 2017 in response to the need for data science training in African countries, particularly those most affected by the SKA (Square Kilometre Array) Telescope. When completed, the SKA will be the world’s largest radio telescope, with a portion located in South Africa. Partner countries across Africa, including Botswana, Ghana, Kenya, Madagascar, Mauritius, Mozambique, Namibia, and Zambia, will form a continental radio telescope network known as the African VLBI Network (AVN).


The aim of DARA Big Data is to equip students from these 8 countries with the skills necessary to translate and analyze the massive amounts of data generated by the telescope. This training extends beyond astronomy to fields such as agriculture, medical sciences, and data-driven policy-making.

The Big Data Africa School

The aim of the School was to teach the students important techniques when working with large data sets, so that they can then apply what they had learnt to their own postgraduate research. To achieve this objective, students were provided access to “real-life” astronomy, healthcare and sustainable agriculture data sets, which they were required to use to answer “real-life” problems.
